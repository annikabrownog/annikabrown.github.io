# Current Research

- **2025–** – *How Psychometric Models Shape Impact and Equity in Early Education RCTs*, Utrecht University, Research Masters Thesis  
  Investigating how measurement models influence the assessment of program impact and equity in an early childhood education RCT in Bulgaria. I find that conclusions can differ depending on the psychometric model used to convert assessment responses into values of children's ability.

- **2023–** – *Are Sanctions Discriminatory?*, Prof. Purba Mukerji, Connecticut College, Researcher  
  Studying whether economic or legal sanctions disproportionately negatively impact low- and middle-income countries.

# Other Research

- **2023–2024** – *Land Use Decisions in Connecticut*, Prof. Ronald Flores, Connecticut College, Researcher  
  Analyzed municipal land use policies to understand patterns of zoning and their effects on housing equity. See [The Room at the Table Report](https://cho.thehousingcollective.org/impact/participation-local-control-land-use-connecticut).

- **2022–2023** – *Ukrainian and Afghan Refugees in Denmark, Sweden, and Iceland’s Labor Markets*, Connecticut College, Senior Honors Thesis  
  Examined labor market outcomes and integration policies for refugees across Nordic countries.

- **2021–2022** – *Empathy in Toddlers: A Cross-Cultural Comparison of Prosocial Actions*, Aarhus University (DK) & Connecticut College (USA), Independent Study  
  Observed and analyzed prosocial behaviors in toddlers across different cultural contexts.

- **2021** – *Financial Inclusion of the Underprivileged: Mobile Money*, Prof. Purba Mukerji, Connecticut College, Research Assistant  
  Assisted in data collection and analysis on mobile money usage and its effects on financial inclusion for low-income communities.
